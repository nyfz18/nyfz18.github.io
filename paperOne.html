The Human Condition: Unconscious Biases

By Nina Zhang

As humans, we have achieved far beyond what we could have ever dreamed of as a species. We've discovered, we've learned, and we've created. We've built civilizations, explored the cosmos, and unlocked the secrets to life itself. However, in pursuit of greatness, it is easy to lose ourselves.

We are human. We are not perfect, and we are not infalliable. While we are capable of boundless creativity, empathy, and reason, we are also governed by unconscious biases that distort our perception and decision-making. These biases, often invisible to us, influence our lives in ways we may not fully understand.

This is a paradox that lies at the heart of our thought and reasoning. How can we build a better world, when our very own thinking is flawed?
